# Competitor Watchlist

> **Purpose**: The 5-7 people or brands whose audiences overlap significantly with yours. Used by the Competitive Analyzer, Content Gap Analyzer, Offer Intel, and Substack Research agents.
>
> **Instructions**: These don't have to be direct competitors. Adjacent creators whose audience follows both of you are equally valuable. The key is audience overlap — do their readers and your readers have the same problems?
>
> **What to include**: Anyone whose content you already track, whose audience you'd like to reach, or who occupies a similar space even with a different angle.

### Stéphanie Walter
- **Website**: stephaniewalter.design
- **Newsletter**: yes, via her website (curated UX and accessibility resources)
- **YouTube**: not a primary channel
- **LinkedIn**: yes, active, 500+ connections
- **Substack**: not used; blog is on her own site
- **Who they target**: UX researchers, designers, and enterprise product teams, particularly in banking, financial, automotive, healthcare, and travel sectors
- **What they sell**: workshops (notably "Accessibility for designers"), conference talks, a book on user journey maps, mentoring
- **Their core message/positioning**: accessibility is the whole team's responsibility, not just developers'; a lot of accessibility issues can be prevented at the design stage, before code is written
- **What they do well**: hands-on, practical workshops with real Figma exercises rather than pure theory; strong reputation in enterprise UX specifically; prolific, curated content output
- **What they miss**: workshop content is explicitly hands-on and design-tool focused rather than strategic; does not cover organisational buy-in or the legal/legislative side in depth
- **Threat level**: High, closest positioning match to Piccia: a designer teaching accessibility to designers, similar audience and workshop format

### Oliver Schöndorfer (Pimp my Type)
- **Website**: pimpmytype.com
- **Newsletter**: Font Friday Newsletter, weekly
- **YouTube**: yes, "Pimp my Type" channel, his primary platform
- **LinkedIn**: active
- **Substack**: not used
- **Who they target**: designers and developers wanting to improve typography, with accessibility as one supporting theme rather than the central focus
- **What they sell**: online courses, workshops, consulting, conference talks
- **Their core message/positioning**: good typography drives usability, accessibility, and brand consistency; accessibility and beautiful, expressive design are compatible, not opposed
- **What they do well**: highly distinctive, entertaining personal brand; consistent weekly content; directly tackles the "accessible means ugly" myth, similar territory to Piccia's own positioning, but through a typography lens specifically
- **What they miss**: typography is the entry point and centre of gravity, not full-spectrum accessibility; does not cover broader accessible design practice, content, or organisational training
- **Threat level**: Medium, adjacent rather than directly overlapping: shares the "accessibility is not a creative limitation" argument, but from a narrower, typography-specific angle
- **Added**: 2026-07-05

### Natalie MacLees (AAArdvark)
- **Website**: aaardvarkaccessibility.com
- **Newsletter**: not confirmed as a primary channel
- **YouTube**: not a primary channel
- **LinkedIn**: active, personal brand as "Natalie Mac"
- **Substack**: not used
- **Who they target**: digital agencies, freelancers, and web development teams, particularly WordPress developers, needing to test and remediate accessibility issues at scale
- **What they sell**: AAArdvark, a paid accessibility testing and issue-tracking SaaS (software as a service) tool, plus audits, monitoring, remediation services, and "The AAArdvark Circle", a paid membership community for freelancers and agencies
- **Their core message/positioning**: accessibility shouldn't be overwhelming or reserved for experts; created "WCAG in Plain English", a free plain-language rewrite of the WCAG success criteria
- **What they do well**: strong product-led offer with a genuinely useful free resource (WCAG in Plain English) as a trust-building front door; deep technical and developer credibility (25 years, former senior UI developer at Sony, co-founder of WordPress Accessibility Day); explicitly rejects overlay tools as a false fix, a position Piccia would agree with
- **What they miss**: developer- and tool-led rather than design-led; does not approach accessibility through a creative or design-process lens; content and community are agency/developer-facing, not aimed at non-specialists or marketers
- **Threat level**: Low to medium, different business model (a SaaS product plus community, not training or speaking) and a different buyer (developers and agencies rather than Piccia's broader non-specialist and design audience), but same overall market and shared audience segment among agencies
- **Added**: 2026-07-05
