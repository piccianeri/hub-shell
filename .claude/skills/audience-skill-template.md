## What Makes Them Trust You

[Trust signals that work for your specific audience. These are NOT generic — figure out what actually works for your people.]

Example:
- Evidence of real builds (screenshots, process walkthroughs, "here's what broke")
- Consistency of showing up (250+ weekly issues signals reliability)
- Specificity to their situation (names their exact reality)
- Anti-hype positioning ("I call BS on..." earns trust faster than claims)
- Proof from people like them (not follower counts)

---

## What Breaks Trust

[Things that make them click away, unsubscribe, or never buy]

Example:
- Urgency-based tactics ("limited time," "doors closing")
- Aspirational lifestyle framing (luxury cars, exotic beaches)
- Follower counts and "featured in" badges
- Overpolished production (too slick = less trustworthy)
- Generic testimonials that could be about any product

## The Sandra Test (rename to your persona's name)

Every piece of content should pass three questions:

1. **Would [NAME] understand this in 10 seconds?** If no → simplify the language or concept.
2. **Would [NAME] trust this?** If no → add proof of real work, remove hype, show the process.
3. **Would [NAME] act on this?** If no → make the next step smaller, more specific, more obviously valuable.

If all three are yes, ship it.
